How Count Check Works

Behind our Discord interface is a highly optimized data processing pipeline. Count Check works continuously to retrieve pricing data, parse gameplay loot tables, and protect your community server’s progression.

The Real-Time Data Pipeline

Every 5 minutes, Count Check connects to the OSRS Wiki's real-time pricing database:

  • Data Cleansing: The engine processes raw high and low prices for over 2,000 items, automatically filtering out inactive items, quest-specific duplicates, and extreme price-manipulation spikes.
  • Background Memory Caching: Rather than making your Discord commands wait to pull historical volume data and calculate complex statistics from a slow database, Count Check processes these calculations in the background. This allows commands like /flip and the new /breakouts to return results instantly without any delay.

Symmetrical Drop Table Parsing

When you run commands like /kill, /open, or /clue, the bot is not simply rolling a random number generator:

  • Structured Drop Tables: The simulator parses highly structured, official OSRS drop table configurations (stored locally as JSON arrays).
  • High-Fidelity Physics: It calculates exact primary rolls, sub-table weights, pre-rolls, and tertiary rolls (such as clues and pets) in the exact order the game engine does. This gives you an incredibly accurate representation of real-world drops.
  • Separated Main and Tertiary Rolls: To ensure true accuracy, the game simulator separates standard drops (which roll multiple times for bosses like Zulrah and Vorkath) from rare tertiary drops (like clues and pets) which are rolled exactly once, preventing duplications.
  • Unique Item Lockout: Enforces Jagex-accurate limits, blocking duplicate clue scrolls or untradeable boss heads from accumulating if you already hold them in your active session.

The Risk-Adjusted Sizer Engine

Count Check uses mathematical risk models, price channel indicators, and volume-velocity filters to generate recommendations:

  • Trailing Moving Volatility: We measure an item's price fluctuations over time, giving more weight to recent price movements. This helps the engine distinguish between natural market noise and genuine price breakouts.
  • Relative Channel Position Index (RCPI): We evaluate where an item’s current price sits on a 14-day floor-to-ceiling scale. If an item is trading too close to its historical ceiling, its Reversion Risk increases, warning you against "buying at the peak" right before a price correction.
  • Shannon's Activity Entropy: We analyze how evenly an item's trade volume is spread across the 24 hours of the day. This automatically filters out illiquid items that are only active during a single peak hour and completely dead during off-peak times.
  • Timezone Density Filter: We calculate how active the market is for an item *right now* in the current UTC hour. If the current hour is an off-peak dead zone, the engine flags it as a risk to protect your capital from locks.
  • F2P Budget Calibration: For free-to-play players, the engine scales down its minimum position profit floor from 15,000 GP to 2,500 GP, making sure low-budget trades remain profitable, accessible, and accurately configured.
  • The "Proven Worthy" Consistency Score: To prevent recommending dead or heavily manipulated items, we analyze how consistently an item's spread has completed successful transactions over the past 24 hours. Items with reliable, repeating spreads are prioritized first.
  • Risk-Weighted Sorting: To keep recommendations safe and user-friendly, your portfolio is sorted by its risk-adjusted profit. Risky, highly volatile trades (C and D grades) have their priority heavily penalized, pushing safe and liquid trades (A and B grades) to the top of your recommendations page.

The Autonomous Trading Emulator

Every 5 minutes, our background trading bot runs simulations to test sizer performance using these real-world competitive rules:

  • Strict G.E. Slot Allocation: The bot strictly respects OSRS's 8-slot Grand Exchange limit. The sum of all active buying and selling orders can never exceed 8, simulating slot-starvation.
  • Capital Partitioning: Operating capital is split into two distinct liquidity buckets:
    • Fast-Churn Bucket (70% of Cash): Allocated to high-volume commodities (runes, ammo, potions). These orders have strict 30-minute force-exit timeouts to keep cash flowing.
    • Value-Invest Bucket (30% of Cash): Reserved for high-margin, slower-moving premium items.
  • Competitive Undercutting: Low-volume items have a 30% chance per cycle of being undercut by other traders. This completely blocks your order from filling during that cycle, simulating active queue competition.
  • Sizing Decay (Partial Fills): Bids do not fill 100% instantly. Orders fill in realistic, fractional waves (15% to 45% fill rates per cycle). The filled portion is split off into a selling slot, while the unfilled portion remains in your buying queue.
  • Volatility-Adjusted Stop Loss (VASL): If market support collapses below a volatility-based threshold, the position is immediately dumped at a loss to protect your starting capital.
  • Tax Realization: The standard rounded-down 2.0% Grand Exchange tax is deducted from all completed transactions.

Local Rate-Limit Protections

Connecting to Jagex’s official databases too quickly will result in your hosting server's IP address being blocked.

  • Anti-Spam Cooldowns: Count Check implements local database timers on stats and highscore commands.
  • Uninterrupted Service: By caching player stats for 3 hours and restricting the frequency of /temple update, the bot ensures your community server remains completely online, responsive, and protected from API firewall blocks.
